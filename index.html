About Me

My academic journey and research philosophy

Academic Background

My journey in computer science began with a passion for understanding how technology can enhance human capabilities while respecting human values. This led me to pursue advanced studies at prestigious institutions:

  • PhD in Computer Science - Stanford University, 2010
  • MSc in Artificial Intelligence - Massachusetts Institute of Technology, 2006
  • BSc in Computer Engineering - University of California, Berkeley, 2004

My doctoral dissertation, "Ethical Considerations in Autonomous Learning Systems," was awarded the Distinguished Dissertation Award and has been cited over 500 times.

Research Philosophy

I believe that technology should serve humanity, not the other way around. My work is guided by the principle that AI systems must be designed with human values at their core. This philosophy informs both my research and teaching approach.

At the Ethical Computing Lab, we focus on three core principles:

  • Transparency: AI systems should be explainable and understandable to users
  • Fairness: AI systems should not perpetuate or amplify existing biases
  • Human-Centered Design: Technology should enhance human capabilities and respect human autonomy

Ethical Considerations in Reinforcement Learning Systems: A Framework for Evaluation

Smith, J., Johnson, A., & Williams, B.
Journal of Artificial Intelligence Research, 2023
Vol. 78, pp. 145-178
DOI: 10.1234/jair.2023.123

This paper explores the ethical implications of deploying reinforcement learning systems in real-world scenarios, proposing a comprehensive framework for evaluating potential harms and benefits. We identify key ethical considerations including safety, fairness, transparency, and value alignment, and develop metrics for assessing RL systems against these criteria. Our framework is validated through case studies in healthcare, autonomous vehicles, and content recommendation systems, demonstrating its applicability across diverse domains.

Transparent Neural Networks: A Novel Approach to Explainable AI

Smith, J., Chen, L., & Davis, M.
IEEE Transactions on Neural Networks and Learning Systems, 2022
Vol. 33, pp. 2145-2163
DOI: 10.1109/TNNLS.2022.3156789

We present a novel architecture for neural networks that provides inherent transparency in decision-making processes without sacrificing performance on complex tasks. Our approach combines attention mechanisms with interpretable feature extraction and decision path visualization to create neural networks whose reasoning can be understood by both technical and non-technical users. Experimental results on image classification, natural language processing, and medical diagnosis tasks demonstrate that our transparent neural networks achieve accuracy comparable to state-of-the-art black-box models while providing human-interpretable explanations for their decisions.

Human-Centered Design Principles for Conversational Agents: An Empirical Evaluation

Smith, J., & Brown, R.
ACM CHI Conference on Human Factors in Computing Systems, 2021
DOI: 10.1145/3411764.3445423
Best Paper Award

This research establishes design principles for conversational agents that prioritize human needs, preferences, and limitations based on extensive user studies. Through a series of experiments with 250 participants across diverse demographics, we identify key factors that influence user trust, satisfaction, and continued engagement with conversational AI systems. Our findings lead to a set of 12 design principles covering transparency, error handling, personality calibration, and cultural sensitivity. We validate these principles through the development and evaluation of a prototype conversational agent, demonstrating significant improvements in user experience metrics compared to conventional approaches.

Teaching Philosophy

My approach to teaching is guided by the belief that education should empower students to become not just skilled practitioners, but thoughtful and ethical contributors to society. In the rapidly evolving field of computer science, I focus on developing both technical excellence and critical awareness of the broader implications of technology.

Integrating Ethics and Technical Content

I believe that ethical considerations should be woven into the fabric of technical education, not treated as an afterthought. In all my courses, from introductory programming to advanced AI, I incorporate discussions of ethical implications, societal impacts, and professional responsibility. This integrated approach helps students develop a holistic understanding of technology and its role in society.

Active and Experiential Learning

I employ active learning strategies that engage students in the material beyond passive listening. Through project-based assignments, case studies, debates, and collaborative problem-solving, students develop deeper understanding and practical skills. I design learning experiences that mirror real-world challenges, preparing students for the complexity they will encounter in their careers.

Inclusive and Diverse Perspectives

I am committed to creating an inclusive learning environment that welcomes diverse perspectives and experiences. I incorporate examples, case studies, and readings from a wide range of contexts and cultures, helping students understand the global impact of technology and the importance of designing for diverse users. I actively work to make my courses accessible to students from all backgrounds.

Fostering Critical Thinking

Beyond teaching technical skills, I challenge students to think critically about technology—to question assumptions, consider alternatives, and evaluate trade-offs. Through Socratic questioning, debates, and reflective assignments, I help students develop the intellectual tools to navigate complex ethical dilemmas and make thoughtful decisions in their professional lives.

"My ultimate goal as an educator is to prepare students not just for their first job, but for a lifetime of ethical and impactful contributions to technology and society. I measure my success not only by what my students know, but by how they think and the values they bring to their work."
